# Data Governance and Access to PII

 Introduction

In any organization, data is not just information stored in a system. It is an important resource that supports planning, reporting, decision-making, and daily operations. Because of this, data must be managed carefully. Data governance is the way an organization controls how data is collected, stored, protected, shared, and used.

In Business Intelligence, data governance is very important because managers and analysts depend on data to understand business performance. If the data is inaccurate, duplicated, incomplete, or accessed by the wrong people, the reports produced from that data may mislead the organization. Good data governance therefore helps to make sure that business decisions are based on information that is reliable and properly protected.

## Meaning of Data Governance

Data governance refers to the rules, responsibilities, and processes that guide how data is handled in an organization. It explains who owns the data, who can access it, how it should be protected, and how its quality should be maintained.

It is not only about technology. It also involves people, policies, and accountability. For example, an organization may have a database system, but without clear rules on who is allowed to edit or view certain records, the data can easily become disorganized or misused.

## Importance of Data Governance in Business Intelligence

Business Intelligence systems are used to turn raw data into useful insights. These insights can help an organization understand sales trends, customer behaviour, financial performance, employee productivity, and many other areas. However, the value of Business Intelligence depends on the quality and safety of the data being used.

Data governance helps Business Intelligence by ensuring that the data is accurate, consistent, secure, and available to the right people. It also reduces confusion because users can understand where the data comes from and whether it can be trusted. When an organization has strong data governance, reports become more reliable and decisions become more informed.

## Personally Identifiable Information

Personally Identifiable Information, also called PII, is any information that can be used to identify a specific person. This type of information is sensitive because it is connected to an individual’s privacy.

Examples of PII include:

- Full name
- Phone number
- Email address
- National identification number
- Passport number
- Student number
- Home address
- Bank details
- Date of birth

If this information is exposed or used wrongly, it can lead to serious problems such as identity theft, fraud, embarrassment, discrimination, or loss of trust in the organization.

## Access to PII

Access to PII should not be open to everyone in an organization. It should only be given to people who genuinely need it to perform their work. This is important because the more people who can access personal information, the higher the risk of misuse or accidental exposure.

For example, a human resource officer may need access to employee personal details, but a sales assistant may not need that same access. In the same way, a student records officer may need to view student numbers and contact details, but another department may only need general statistics without seeing personal information.

This approach is known as limiting access based on roles and responsibilities. It helps to protect privacy and reduces unnecessary exposure of sensitive data.

## Key Principles of Data Governance

### Data Quality

Data should be correct, complete, consistent, and updated when necessary. Poor-quality data can lead to wrong reports and poor business decisions.

### Data Security

Data should be protected from unauthorized access, loss, damage, or theft. Security measures may include passwords, encryption, backups, and secure login systems.

### Data Privacy

Personal information should be collected and used only for clear and legitimate reasons. Organizations should avoid collecting information that they do not need.

### Data Ownership

There should be clear responsibility for every important set of data. A data owner ensures that the data is properly managed, corrected, and protected.

### Access Control

Not every user should have access to all information. Access should depend on a person’s role, duties, and level of authority.

### Compliance

Organizations must follow relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies that deal with data protection and privacy.

## How Organizations Can Protect Sensitive Data

Organizations can protect sensitive data in several practical ways. First, they can use role-based access control so that users only see the information needed for their work. Second, they can use strong passwords and multi-factor authentication to reduce unauthorized access. Third, sensitive data can be encrypted so that it is difficult to read if stolen.

Organizations can also keep audit logs to monitor who accessed data and what actions were performed. Regular training is also important because many data problems happen when employees do not understand how to handle information safely.

## Conclusion

Data governance is important because it brings order, responsibility, and protection to the way an organization handles data. In Business Intelligence, it helps ensure that reports and analysis are based on trustworthy information. It also protects sensitive information, especially PII, from misuse or unauthorized access.

A strong data governance approach allows an organization to make better decisions while respecting privacy, security, and accountability..
